AD

Aurelio Dioval writes the way storms arrive — without apology. Born between cultures and raised on the edge of silence, he builds worlds where archangels walk among humans, where love is both forbidden and holy, and where the body is scripture no government can censor.

His debut novel, The Light of Woza, launched a dark fantasy saga of discipline, power, and the breaking of balance — now available on Kindle. Writing as Mr. Ex The Last Poet, his poetry collection Wine, Sea, and Fire fuses three ancient rivers — the Persian fire of Rumi, the Arabian ink of Qabbani, and the Portuguese sea of Camões — into a voice that burns whether or not anyone is watching.

When he is not writing, he is building — architecting technology with the same precision he brings to prose. Code and verse share a spine: structure that serves beauty.

Origin

Chapter I

The Beginning

Born between cultures, raised on the edge of silence. Language was never just a tool — it was survival. Before there were books, there were letters never sent, words swallowed to keep the peace, and stories whispered to no one.

Chapter II

The Builder

Technology came first. Not as passion but as discipline. The architecture of systems — databases, servers, interfaces — taught a fundamental truth: structure serves beauty. Code taught patience. Debugging taught humility. Shipping taught courage.

Chapter III

The Poet Awakens

Mr. Ex The Last Poet was not chosen. The name arrived the way storms arrive — without permission. Poetry became the place where the unspeakable could finally speak. Rumi showed the door. Qabbani kicked it open. Camões stood in the ocean and refused to drown. The voice that emerged from these three rivers was something new.

Chapter IV

The Novelist

The Light of Woza began as a question: What happens when the most powerful being in existence is ordered to love? Aurelio Dioval was born to answer it. Not a pen name — an identity. The one who builds kingdoms on paper and burns them down with truth.

Chapter V

Now

Washington State. Still building. Still burning. Book One is live. Book Two is complete. Wine, Sea, and Fire is being forged. The empire is rising — one line, one system, one story at a time.

THE KINGDOM OF WOZA

The Light of Woza

A Tale of Two Destinies

Book One

In a world where power is never allowed to exist without balance, Woza stands apart. It is not a kingdom built on conquest, but on discipline — where every ruler stands beside a shadow, and every decision is measured against a higher order known only as the Light.

For generations, this system has held. Until something changes. Sarif is not meant to be different. He is meant to serve — silent, unseen, and bound to protect. But from the moment he is placed within the royal structure of Woza, it becomes clear: He is not like the others.

He does not follow the pattern. He does not fit within the system. And for the first time in its history... Woza begins to shift. Bound to Princess Anna not as a follower, but as something closer to an equal — Sarif's existence challenges the very foundation of balance itself.

Beyond the borders of Woza, ambition grows unchecked. A rising force seeks power without discipline. Control without restraint. And a darkness begins to spread — one that does not destroy... but corrupts.

Selected Verses

When the Soul Speaks

Do not ask if I am whole. The wine glass is cracked but I still offer what remains. I am not healed. I am not pure. I am the temple after the fire and you are asking if the flame still dances. It does. But only for those who kneel barefoot. If you wish to love me do not come for the incense. Come for the ashes.

— Mr. Ex The Last Poet, from Section I: The Reed's Lament

I Don't Ask Permission

I don't ask permission to adore you. Love, in its purest form, is disobedient. I didn't rehearse this. I didn't plan the trembling or the way your name rewrites my pulse. Darling, I was not built to wait. I am not prayer — I am fate.

— Mr. Ex The Last Poet, from Section II: The Body's Scripture

The Creed of My Blood and Breath

I am no more than dust suspended by grace — but this dust has teeth. I do not ask the sky for permission to rise. I do not beg the sun for its warmth. I stand because the One who made me did not make me to kneel to anything that is not Him. This is not a poem. This is not a performance. This is my blood. This is my breath. And the angels have written it down.

— Mr. Ex The Last Poet, from Section III: The Navigator's Saudade

"These poems were born in the collision of three rivers — Persian fire, Arabian ink, and Portuguese sea. They belong to no one woman and every soul that has burned."

— Mr. Ex The Last Poet
